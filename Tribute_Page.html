Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Great Indian Scientist and Politician who played
a leading role in the development of India's missile and
nuclear weapons programs.

About The Legend

  1. A.P.J Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam,was born on October 15,1931,in Rameshwaram,Tamil Nadu,India.

  2. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

  3. Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Defense Research and Development Oraganisation(DRDO).

  4. In 1969,he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of the SLV -III,the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India.

  5. Rejoining DRDO in 1982,Kalam planned the program that produced the program that produced a no. of successfull missiles, which helped earn him the nickname "Missile Man"

  6. Among those successes was Agni, India's first intermediate range ballistic missile,which incorporated aspects of the SLV - III and was launched in 1989.

  7. He also played a pivotal,organisational,technical and political role in India's Pokhran - II nuclear test 1998,the first since the originalnuclear test by India in 1974.

  8. From 1992 to 1997 Kalam was scientific adviserr to the defense minister, and he later seved as principal scientific aviser(1999-2001) to the government with the rank of cabinet minister.

  9. His prominent role in the country's 1998 nuclear weapons tests solidified India as a nuclear power and establishied Kalam as a national hero, although the test caused great concern in the international community.

  10. In 1998 Kalam put forwarrd a countrywidw plan called Technology Vision 2020,which he described as a roadmap for transforming India from a less developed to a developed society in 20 years. The plan called for, among other measures,increasing agricultural productivity,emphasizing technology as a vehicle for economic growth,and widening access to health care and education

  11. Kalam recieved 7 honorary doctorates from 40 universities. The Government of India honoured him with the Padma Bhushanin 1981 and the Padma Vibhushanin 1900 for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.

  12. In 1997, Kalam recieved India's highest civilian honour,the Bharat Ratna,for his contribution to the scientific researrch and modernisation of sdefence technolog in India.

  13. In 2013,hewas the recipient of the Van Braun Award from the National Space Society to recognise excellence in the management and leadership of a space related project.

  14. While delivering a lecture at Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Kalam collapsed and died from an apparent cardiac arrest on 27July 2015aged 83.

  15. Wheeler Island,a national missile test in Odisha,was renamed Kalam Island in september 2015.

  16. In February 2018,scientists from the Botanical Survey of India named a newly found plant species as Drypetes kalamii,in his honour.
